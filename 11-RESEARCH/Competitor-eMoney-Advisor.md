# Key Takeaways for Advisor Intelligence

1. **eMoney's complexity is our opportunity**: Advisors report 3-6 months to get up to speed with eMoney, and it's "excessively complex" for most users. Our copy-paste templates require zero training — position this hard.
2. **CoPlanner AI reduces plan-building time by 48%**: eMoney's new AI feature generates personalized planning actions in seconds. While impressive, it's locked inside their $4,500+/yr platform — our $49.99/mo gives any advisor AI capabilities immediately.
3. **Inflexible 12-month contracts** create switching pain. Our month-to-month pricing with no lock-in is a clear advantage for cautious buyers.
4. **eMoney + Zocks integration** shows the ecosystem is consolidating — AI notetakers feeding data into planning platforms. Advisor Intelligence sits outside this pipeline, serving the communication and content layer these integrations don't touch.
5. **eMoney is owned by Fidelity Investments** — enterprise backing but also enterprise bureaucracy. We can move faster and respond to advisors directly.

# eMoney Advisor

## Overview
eMoney Advisor is one of the three dominant financial planning software platforms (alongside RightCapital and MoneyGuide). Owned by Fidelity Investments since 2015, eMoney offers comprehensive financial planning, account aggregation, client portal, and now AI-powered features. Used by thousands of advisory firms, particularly at the enterprise level. In March 2026, eMoney launched CoPlanner, an AI feature that generates financial plan strategies in seconds.

## Pricing
- **Quote-based pricing** — no published standard rates
- **Estimated range**: $3,500-$4,500+/year per advisor (based on user reports of ~$379/month billing)
- **Plans**: Plus, Pro, Premier, Enterprise tiers
- **Annual commitment**: 12-month contracts required
- **CoPlanner AI**: Included with existing subscriptions (no additional cost)
- Historically the most expensive of the "big three" planning platforms

## Target Market & Firm Size Sweet Spot
- Mid-to-large RIAs and wealth management firms
- Enterprise broker-dealers and wirehouses (Fidelity distribution channel)
- Firms wanting deep account aggregation and client portal
- Advisors doing comprehensive cash-flow-based financial planning
- NOT ideal for solo advisors or small firms (cost and complexity)

## Product Features

### Core Platform
- Comprehensive financial planning (retirement, tax, estate, insurance, education, Social Security)
- Cash-flow-based planning engine (key differentiator)
- Client portal with account aggregation
- Interactive what-if scenarios
- Monte Carlo simulation
- White-labeled client reports
- Estate planning flowcharts
- Collaborative planning tools

### AI Features (2026)
- **CoPlanner**: AI generates personalized planning actions to achieve advisor-defined probability of success. Evaluates income, assets, tax considerations, retirement timing, spending. Produces multiple structured strategies for review. Beta showed 48% reduction in plan-building time. Built on structured, rules-based logic (not LLM hallucination-prone). Developed by in-house CFP professionals.
- **Zocks Integration**: AI-powered document intelligence — extracts client data from financial documents and populates eMoney fields (income, assets, beneficiaries, account numbers)
- **Wealth.com Integration**: AI-powered estate planning data sync — eliminates manual data entry for estate documents

### Integrations
- Zocks (AI meeting notes → plan data)
- Jump (meeting automation)
- Wealth.com (estate planning)
- Major CRMs (Salesforce, Redtail, Wealthbox)
- Custodians (Fidelity, Schwab, Pershing)

## Weaknesses & Gaps (what advisors complain about)
- **Excessive complexity**: "Offering a multitude of features that most financial advisors will find overwhelming and ultimately underutilized" — 3-6 months to master
- **Data integration issues**: "Clients always have to update connections due to changes in financial institutions' password update rules" — aggregation is unreliable
- **Inflexible contracts**: 12-month commitment, "impossible to cancel despite the software being unsuitable"
- **Limited estate planning**: "The estate planning features are somewhat limited" — ironic for a comprehensive platform
- **Cost**: "Pricey to get all the features" — premium pricing creates barrier
- **Lower satisfaction**: RightCapital scored higher in 9 of 13 sub-categories (Kitces Research 2025)
- **Clunky interface**: "Can be clunky at times, particularly with data entry and integration issues"
- **No content creation**: No templates for client communication, prospecting, or marketing
- **No self-serve onboarding**: Requires demo, custom quote, lengthy implementation

## How Advisor Intelligence Differentiates
1. **Instant value vs. 6-month ramp** — Our templates work the moment you sign up. eMoney takes 3-6 months to master.
2. **90% cheaper** — $49.99/mo ($600/yr) vs $3,500-$4,500+/yr. No annual lock-in.
3. **Content creation focus** — eMoney builds financial plans. We help advisors communicate those plans to clients, prospect new clients, and create marketing content.
4. **Zero data requirements** — eMoney needs full client financial data + account aggregation connections. We require zero client data on our platform.
5. **Month-to-month flexibility** — No 12-month contracts, no cancellation friction.
6. **Platform-agnostic** — Works alongside eMoney, RightCapital, MoneyGuide, or no planning software at all. We complement, not compete.
7. **Complementary tool** — eMoney users building comprehensive plans still need help explaining those plans to clients, writing follow-up emails, creating educational content, and prospecting new clients. That's our entire product.
