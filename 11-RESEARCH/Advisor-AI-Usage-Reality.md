# What Financial Advisors ACTUALLY Use AI For: Comprehensive Research Report

**Research Date:** March 14, 2026
**Sources:** 20+ industry surveys, trade publications, advisor forums, and vendor data
**Key Surveys Analyzed:** Schwab RIA & AI Study (2026), T3/Inside Information Software Survey (2025/2026), Advisor360 Connected Wealth Report (2025/2026), Horsesmouth Advisor-AI Usage Survey (2025), Kitces AdvisorTech Research, LPL Focus Survey (2025), EY GenAI in Wealth & Asset Management Survey (2025), ACA/NSCP AI Benchmarking Survey (2025), Betterment Advisor Survey (2025)

---

## 1. WHAT ADVISORS ACTUALLY USE AI FOR (Ranked by Frequency)

### Tier 1: Dominant Use Cases (Used by majority of AI-adopting advisors)

**1. Meeting Notes, Summaries & CRM Updates**
- The single most common AI use case across every survey
- Schwab (2026): Use cases "concentrated on administrative tasks like notetaking and email drafting"
- Kitces Research: AI meeting prep + follow-up takes an average of 1 hour each (2 hours total) per meeting without AI notetakers
- Jump AI user quote: "With Jump, what used to be 40 minutes of typing meeting notes, organizing tasks, and posting to CRM, we now get 95% done in 3-4 minutes."
- T3/Inside Information 2026 Survey: AI notetaking market penetration reached 42.86% of advisors
- Tools dominating: Jump, Zocks, Focal, Zeplyn, Finmate AI, Zoom AI Companion, Fireflies
- Financial Planning (2025): "AI made major inroads into financial advisor technology stacks. Prospecting and meeting note-taking were positives."

**2. Email Drafting & Client Communication**
- Schwab (2026): Among the top use cases alongside notetaking
- Horsesmouth (2024): Advisors report "significant time savings and improved efficiency fleshing out ideas, writing emails, and creating marketing copy"
- Capital Group: Advisors use apps like Vega Minds that "automatically create draft replies to emails" and "learn your writing style with a given client over time"
- Kitces: "The first clear use case for financial advisors is to leverage ChatGPT as a form of 'generative AI' to create an initial first draft of client communication"
- Financial Planning: A Morningstar study found clients were less likely to pay more for advisors who used AI to write personal emails, suggesting a tension between efficiency and authenticity

**3. Content Creation & Marketing**
- Horsesmouth (2025): Advisors use AI to simplify complex financial topics (25%), create educational content (25%), and brainstorm marketing strategies (24%)
- SmartAsset/T3 (2025): "Just over 35% of advisors cite ChatGPT as their most-used tool"
- Advisors use AI for: blog posts, newsletters, social media content, LinkedIn posts, video scripts
- RFG Advisory: "AI can help you brainstorm post ideas, draft captions in your brand voice, and transform longer content like blogs or newsletters into shorter pieces for LinkedIn or Instagram"
- Kitces (Jan 2025): "Since the wide release of ChatGPT in 2022, advisors have used AI for a multitude of writing tasks ranging from blog posts to client meeting notes to follow-up emails"

**4. Meeting Prep & Pre-Meeting Briefs**
- Tools like Jump, Zocks, and Focal automatically pull CRM data, past meeting notes, and client context to generate pre-meeting preparation briefs
- GReminders: "advisory offices spend approximately 13.5 hours per client per year on" meeting lifecycle activities
- Vanguard: Advisors should focus on "practical productivity wins, such as using AI tools to support client interactions, automate meeting prep, record notes, and draft follow-ups"
- Zeplyn: "Zeplyn gathers all the details you need -- your client's financial goals, status of action items, topics to discuss, conversation starters and more"

### Tier 2: Growing Use Cases (Used by a significant minority)

**5. Research & Staying Current on Industry Trends**
- Betterment (2025): "53% of advisors now use AI to stay current with industry updates, surpassing social media (48% in 2025, down from 59% in 2024)"
- Advisors use ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude to summarize research reports, understand regulatory changes, and track market news
- Morgan Stanley's AI @ Morgan Stanley Assistant: gives 16,000+ financial advisors a knowledge chatbot to query 100,000+ internal research documents

**6. Prospecting & Lead Generation**
- Advisor360 (2025): Prospecting named alongside administrative assistance as a key AI use case
- FINNY AI raised $4.2M to automate personalized outreach to prospects
- Catchlight uses AI to analyze and enrich lead data, assigning quality scores
- Financial Planning (2025): "Prospecting and meeting note-taking were positives"

**7. Predictive Analytics**
- Advisor360 (2025): Named as one of the top three Gen AI use cases alongside marketing and meeting notes
- Used primarily by enterprise wealth management firms with dedicated AI tools
- Includes churn risk indicators, identifying client life events, and surfacing held-away asset opportunities

**8. Summarizing Documents & Research**
- Advisorpedia: "ChatGPT can help financial advisors quickly summarize and shorten lengthy documents"
- Claude specifically praised for "summarizing long agreements, reports, or compliance-heavy text"
- Used for: tax returns (via FP Alpha), estate planning documents, lengthy regulatory updates

### Tier 3: Emerging / Niche Use Cases

**9. Compliance Pre-Checks (early stage)**
- Financial Planning: "Several advisors said they want AI tools that could cut down the time spent on compliance and regulatory tasks"
- Betterment/Considine: AI integration into compliance workflows "is further away"
- ACA/NSCP (2025): Only 28% of firms test or validate AI outputs
- Hadrius specifically built for compliance assistance

**10. Task & Workflow Automation**
- Post-meeting task assignment to team members
- CRM workflow triggers based on meeting summaries
- Automated follow-up scheduling

**11. HR & Internal Operations**
- Financial Planning: AI provides value within HR for "writing job descriptions and conducting behavioral assessments"

## 2. WHAT ADVISORS EXPLICITLY REJECT AI FOR (With Reasons)

### Tasks Advisors Will NOT Use AI For

**1. Personalized Financial Plans**
- Advisor360 (2025): Just 29% use AI for developing personalized financial plans -- the LOWEST of all use cases measured
- Darren Tedesco, President of Advisor360: "Advisors prefer to be hands-on when it comes to tasks that are core to client service"
- Reason: Fiduciary liability, complexity of individual situations, existing dedicated software (eMoney, RightCapital, MoneyGuidePro)

**2. Investment Recommendations & Portfolio Construction**
- Vanguard framework: "AI erodes the advisor's advantage in analytical intelligence" but clients still want human advisors for decisions tied to emotion
- Reddit r/CFP: "the synthesis of information combined with psychological touch of a human advisor is not something it will ever likely be capable of"
- Reason: SEC/FINRA regulatory requirements, fiduciary duty, existing portfolio management software, rebalancing tools, model marketplaces

**3. Direct Client-Facing Advice**
- Northwestern Mutual (2025): "By a wide margin, most Americans today continue to trust financial advisors with their money management over artificial intelligence tools alone"
- Advisors keep AI firmly in back-office/middle-office roles, not client-facing
- Reason: Trust, liability, relationship damage, compliance risk

**4. Tax Planning & Complex Calculations**
- Dedicated tools already exist: Holistiplan (tax planning), FP Alpha (document analysis)
- Mark Holder (MILE Wealth): "I've learned not to ask Chetgee [ChatGPT] questions where the 'average' answer found on the internet is questionable"
- Reddit users note ChatGPT provides incorrect tax bracket information
- Reason: Accuracy requirements, existing specialized software, regulatory liability

**5. Compliance Decisions**
- Financial Planning (2025): "AI use was far and away the most common concern for compliance officers in the wealth management industry" (2025 Investment Management Compliance Testing Survey)
- Firms are reluctant to let AI make compliance determinations
- ACA/NSCP (2025): Only 28% test or validate AI outputs despite 71% formally using AI
- Reason: Regulatory risk, accuracy concerns, audit trail requirements

**6. Estate Planning Legal Documents**
- Dedicated tools exist: Vanilla, Wealth.com, EncorEstate Plans
- AI can analyze existing documents but advisors don't trust it to create legally binding plans
- Reason: Legal liability, state-specific requirements, existing specialized tools

### Why Advisors Reject These Use Cases

| Rejection Reason | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Fiduciary liability** | Financial plans, investment recommendations, tax advice |
| **Existing regulated systems** | Portfolio management, rebalancing, financial planning software, compliance monitoring |
| **Accuracy concerns / hallucinations** | Tax calculations, legal documents, specific financial data |
| **Client trust** | Direct advice, personal relationship moments, behavioral coaching |
| **Compliance/regulatory risk** | Client-facing communications (without review), marketing materials, recordkeeping |
| **Data privacy** | Client PII in general-purpose AI tools, confidential financial data |

## 3. THE GAP: Where AI Adds Real Value

The critical insight: **Advisors use AI for tasks where NO dedicated, regulated system exists.** The gap is in the connective tissue between systems -- the human labor that glues the advisor's day together.

### The Gap Map

| Task Category | Dedicated Software Exists? | AI Fills the Gap? | Why AI Wins Here |
|---|---|---|---|
| Meeting notes & CRM updates | No (manual process) | YES | No regulated system existed; was purely manual labor |
| Email drafting & follow-ups | No (manual writing) | YES | Time-consuming creative work with no dedicated tool |
| Content marketing & social media | Partial (FMG, Snappy Kraken) | YES | AI dramatically reduces creation time |
| Meeting prep briefs | No (manual CRM review) | YES | Required manually pulling from multiple systems |
| Prospecting outreach | Partial (lead gen tools) | YES | Personalized messaging at scale was impossible |
| Research summarization | No (manual reading) | YES | Condensing large volumes of text is AI's strength |
| Portfolio management | YES (Orion, Black Diamond, Addepar) | No | Regulated, audited, existing tools work well |
| Financial planning | YES (eMoney, RightCapital, MoneyGuidePro) | No | Fiduciary liability, complex personalization |
| Tax planning | YES (Holistiplan, FP Alpha) | No | Accuracy-critical, state-specific, regulated |
| Compliance monitoring | YES (firm systems, ACA tools) | No | Regulatory requirement for human oversight |
| Rebalancing | YES (iRebal, Orion, tRx) | No | Automated already; regulated |
| Trading & execution | YES (custodian platforms) | No | Highly regulated, existing infrastructure |
| Estate planning documents | YES (Vanilla, Wealth.com) | No | Legal liability, state-specific |

### The Key Pattern

**Where advisors adopt AI fastest:** Tasks that are:
1. Time-consuming but not complex (meeting notes, email drafts)
2. Creative but repetitive (content marketing, social media)
3. Cross-system (pulling data from CRM for meeting prep)
4. Non-regulated (internal communications, brainstorming)
5. Low-risk if imperfect (first drafts that get human review)

**Where advisors refuse AI:** Tasks that are:
1. Fiduciary-bound (investment advice, financial plans)
2. Already served by dedicated software (portfolio management, tax planning)
3. Compliance-monitored (client-facing communications without review)
4. Accuracy-critical with no room for error (tax calculations, legal documents)
5. Core to the client relationship (empathy, behavioral coaching, trust-building)

## 6. STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS FOR ADVISOR INTELLIGENCE

### What This Research Validates About the Product

1. **Copy-paste prompt templates are the RIGHT model.** Advisors want AI to "expedite in a non-automated way" (Kitces). They want to stay in the loop. Templates that advisors copy into Copilot/ChatGPT match exactly how the majority of advisors are actually using AI -- through individual experimentation with general-purpose tools, not firm-wide systems.

2. **The gap between AI notetakers and template-based communication tools is real.** AI notetakers handle the meeting lifecycle. But advisors still need help with the communication and planning tasks AROUND the meeting -- client education materials, prospecting emails, practice management frameworks, and compliance-aware messaging. This is exactly where Advisor Intelligence's 61 templates live.

3. **Content marketing is a huge and growing use case.** With 25% of advisors already using AI for educational content and 24% for marketing strategies (Horsesmouth 2025), the demand for ready-made, compliance-aware content templates is validated.

4. **Advisors explicitly DO NOT want AI for financial planning.** Only 29% use AI for personalized plans (Advisor360). This validates Advisor Intelligence's positioning as a COMMUNICATION and PRODUCTIVITY tool, not a financial planning or investment advice tool.

5. **Microsoft Copilot is a legitimate primary tool.** Dutch wealth management firm Van Lanschot Kempen uses Copilot. Microsoft Teams with Copilot is being adopted across wirehouses. Advisors use Copilot in Outlook, Word, and Teams daily. Positioning the product around Copilot (not just ChatGPT) is strategically sound.

6. **The confidence gap is the real opportunity.** Only 11% of advisors describe themselves as "very confident" in applying AI (Horsesmouth 2025). Nearly 1 in 5 still don't use AI at all. Ready-made templates with clear instructions reduce the confidence barrier.

### Categories That Match Highest-Demand Use Cases

| Advisor Intelligence Category | Matches Which AI Use Case |
|---|---|
| Client Communication | #2 Email drafting (Schwab top use case) |
| Prospecting | #6 Lead gen & outreach (Advisor360 key use case) |
| Marketing Content | #3 Content creation (Horsesmouth 25% usage) |

| Client Education | Simplifying complex topics (Horsesmouth 25%) |
| Practice Management | Internal operations, HR, workflows |

| Onboarding | New client communication & documentation |

### The Biggest Unserved Need

**Post-meeting follow-up communications and client-specific action summaries.** While AI notetakers handle the raw notes, advisors still need help converting those notes into polished, compliance-aware client communications. The bridge between "AI meeting notes" and "personalized client email" is where templates add the most value.
