# Key Takeaways

1. **Michael Kitces is the undisputed center of the advisor universe on LinkedIn.** His blog reaches 200K+ unique visitors monthly, his Members Section serves 20K+ advisors, and he coined the term #AdvisorTech. Engaging with Kitces content is the single highest-leverage LinkedIn activity for Advisor Intelligence.

2. **Samantha Russell grew to 42K+ LinkedIn followers by spending 80-90% of her LinkedIn time commenting on others' posts.** She is the blueprint for advisor marketing on LinkedIn and her FMG Suite webinars specifically teach this strategy. Engaging with her content positions AI in front of advisor marketing decision-makers.

3. **The "Waffle House strategy" works: show up consistently at the same 5-8 profiles, commenting thoughtfully, and their audiences will start recognizing and following you.** This organic engagement approach is more effective than posting alone, especially for new accounts with small followings.

4. **Diversity voices (Yarnway, Dorsainvil) represent the next generation of advisor influence.** These leaders have highly engaged communities that are underserved by traditional fintech. Engaging here positions Advisor Intelligence as inclusive and forward-thinking.

5. **The best time to comment is within the first 60 minutes of an influencer's post.** Early comments on high-engagement posts get more visibility. Set up notifications for top-priority influencers.

# "Waffle House" Engagement Strategy

The concept: show up consistently at the same profiles — like a regular at a diner — until their audience recognizes you. This is the most effective organic growth strategy for new LinkedIn profiles.

## Daily Engagement Routine (15-20 minutes/day)

### Priority 1 — Every Day (set notifications ON)
- **Michael Kitces** — Comment within 60 min of his posts. Add data, a specific example, or a respectful contrarian view.
- **Samantha Russell** — Comment with actionable add-ons to her marketing tips. Share AI-specific applications of her advice.

### Priority 2 — 3-4 Times Per Week
- **Matt Halloran** — Engage on personal branding and communication posts. Connect AI efficiency to his philosophy.
- **Taylor Schulte** — Engage especially on San Diego-local content and tax/retirement topics.
- **Carl Richards** — Comment on sketch posts with simple, clear applications of his concepts to AI.

### Priority 3 — 2-3 Times Per Week
- **Dasarte Yarnway** — Engage on community and accessibility posts. Align with democratization messaging.
- **Justin Castelli** — Engage on life-design and purpose-driven planning content.
- **Rianka Dorsainvil** — Engage on diversity and accessibility posts.

## Comment Quality Rules
1. **Never be promotional** in comments on influencer posts. No links, no pitches.
2. **Add genuine value** — share a specific example, data point, or perspective.
3. **Ask a follow-up question** — this increases chances of a reply (which boosts your visibility).
4. **Be the first commenter** — early comments on high-engagement posts get maximum visibility.
5. **Reply to other commenters** — this creates threads that the algorithm rewards.
6. **Use your real photo and clear headline** — every comment is a mini-profile impression.
