# Product Strategy Pivot — Template Audit & Gap-Filler Positioning

## Executive Summary

Based on in-person coaching sessions with financial advisors at Morgan Stanley, Wells Fargo, and independent RIAs, we've discovered a critical insight that changes our entire product strategy: **advisors will not use AI for tasks where they already have regulated, monitored systems.** They have Holistiplan for tax planning, eMoney for financial planning, Riskalyze for risk assessment, and compliance departments for supervision. Having templates for "Roth Conversion Analysis" or "Retirement Readiness Assessment" on our platform signals that we don't understand their world — and makes the genuinely valuable templates harder to find.

**The pivot**: Reorganize from 11 planning-function categories to 8 workflow categories. Remove 13 templates that overlap with existing systems. Reframe 18 templates from "analysis" framing to "communication/meeting prep" framing. Add ~15 new templates that fill genuine workflow gaps. Position Advisor Intelligence as "the AI layer for everything your existing systems don't cover."

## 6. How This Changes the Demo Flow

### Current Demo (4 scenes with personas)
Shows templates organized by financial planning function. Includes "estate planning" and analysis-type templates.

### Proposed New Demo Flow

**Scene 1: "Before the Meeting"**
Sarah, an advisor at a mid-size RIA, has a client annual review in 2 hours. She opens Meeting Prep → pastes her CRM notes and last meeting summary → gets a comprehensive meeting brief with agenda, talking points, anticipated questions, and risk radar. Time: 30 seconds instead of 45 minutes.

**Scene 2: "After the Meeting"**
Meeting just ended. Sarah opens Follow-Up & CRM → pastes her rough notes → gets a polished follow-up email, compliance-ready CRM note, and action item tracker. She sends the email before the next meeting starts. Time: 2 minutes instead of 40 minutes.

Sarah met a CPA at a conference. She opens Prospecting → COI Outreach → inputs the context → gets a professional introduction email with follow-up sequence. Time: 3 minutes instead of 30 minutes.

## 9. Risk Assessment

### What We Lose
- **13 templates** that took development time to build
- **3 categories** (Tax Planning, Retirement Planning, Deep Analysis) that add to our "template count" marketing
- **Some early-career advisors** who don't have planning software might miss the analysis templates

### What We Gain
- **Sharper positioning** that resonates with experienced advisors ($500M+ AUM)
- **Better credibility** — stops the "this tool doesn't understand my world" reaction
- **Clearer value proposition** — fills gaps no competitor covers
- **Higher usage frequency** — meeting prep and follow-up templates are used daily vs. analysis templates used rarely
- **Differentiation from $363M+ in funded competitors** — we're not trying to be another planning/analysis tool
- **Easier sales conversation** — "we complement your existing stack" vs. "we do everything"

### Mitigation for Risks
1. **Lost template count**: Net gain from 61 → 68 templates (we're adding more than we remove)
2. **Early-career advisors**: Client Education category retains educational versions of removed concepts (Roth Conversion Explainer, Social Security Options Explainer). We're not removing the knowledge — just the "analysis" framing.
3. **Existing user disruption**: With only 4 users in the database, disruption is minimal. Better to make the change now than after scale.
