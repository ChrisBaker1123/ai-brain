# AI Gap Analysis — Where AI Adds Real Value for Financial Advisors

> **Core Insight**: Advisors will NOT use AI for tasks where they already have regulated, monitored systems. They WILL use AI for the gaps between their systems — the 10+ hours per week spent on communication, documentation, and content that no existing software covers.

---

## The Two Worlds: Systems vs. Gaps

### COVERED — Dedicated Systems Already Handle These

| Advisory Function | Leading Systems | Why AI Is Redundant |
|-------------------|----------------|---------------------|
| Portfolio Construction & Management | Orion, Black Diamond, Envestnet, Tamarac | Regulated, audited, integrated with custodians |
| Portfolio Rebalancing | iRebal, Tamarac, Orion Rebalancing | Automated rule-based systems with compliance tracking |
| Financial Planning | eMoney, MoneyGuidePro, RightCapital, Conquest | Core workflow tool, Monte Carlo, cash flow projections |
| Tax Planning & Analysis | Holistiplan (30K users, 42% adoption), FP Alpha, BNA | OCR-based, regulation-current, integrated with planning |
| Risk Assessment | Riskalyze/Nitrogen (30K advisors), Orion Risk Intelligence | Standardized questionnaires, compliance-ready reports |
| Performance Reporting | Custodian reports, Orion, Black Diamond, Addepar | GIPS-compliant, auditable, client portal integrated |
| Compliance Monitoring | RegEd, SMARSH, firm compliance departments | Mandatory, regulated, supervised by CCO |
| Trading & Execution | Custodian platforms (Schwab, Fidelity, Pershing) | Regulated, real-time, best execution requirements |
| Client Portal | Orion Connect, Black Diamond, eMoney client portal | Integrated with planning and portfolio systems |
| Account Aggregation | ByAllAccounts, Quovo/Plaid, Yodlee | API-based, real-time data feeds |

**Key insight**: An advisor at Morgan Stanley with 15 years of experience and $500M under management sees a template called "Roth Conversion Analysis" or "Portfolio Review Report" and thinks: *"This tool doesn't understand my world. I have software for this."* Having these templates on the platform **hurts credibility** more than it helps.

### THE GAP — No Dedicated System Exists for These

| Workflow Gap | What Advisors Do Today | Time Spent | AI Template Value |
|-------------|----------------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Meeting Prep** | Manually pull context from CRM, portfolio system, notes; build agenda | 1+ hour per meeting | Generates comprehensive brief from context paste |
| **Post-Meeting Follow-Up Emails** | Write from scratch or use generic templates | 30-60 min per meeting | Generates personalized email + action items from notes |
| **CRM Note Writing** | Type notes from memory, often incomplete | 15-40 min per meeting | Structures raw notes into compliance-ready CRM entry |
| **Client Email Drafting** | Write from scratch for each situation | 15-25 min per email | Generates situation-specific, tone-matched emails |
| **Market Volatility Communication** | Scramble to draft during market events | 1-3 hours (time-critical) | Generates calm, compliant market communication in minutes |
| **Prospecting Outreach** | Write cold emails, COI letters from scratch | 1-2 hours per outreach | Generates professional outreach with relationship context |
| **LinkedIn/Social Content** | Many advisors skip entirely due to time | 1-2 hours per post | Generates advisor-appropriate social content |
| **Newsletter/Blog Content** | Often outsourced or skipped | 2-3 hours per article | Generates draft content with compliance awareness |
| **Client Education Materials** | Explain concepts verbally, no written materials | 30-60 min per topic | Translates jargon into client-friendly explanations |
| **Difficult Conversation Scripts** | Wing it or avoid the conversation | Unmeasurable anxiety | Provides structured scripts for fees, underperformance, etc. |
| **Onboarding Communication** | Ad hoc emails, inconsistent experience | 3-5 hours per new client | Generates welcome packages, checklists, sequences |
| **Practice Management Docs** | Team meetings without agendas, no SOPs | 1-2 hours per week | Generates agendas, SOPs, process documentation |

## Why the Gap Exists

### 1. CRM Systems Don't Write — They Store
Redtail, Wealthbox, and Salesforce store client data and track activities. But none of them help advisors **write** the email, **draft** the CRM note, or **create** the meeting prep brief. The CRM is a database, not a writing assistant.

### 2. Planning Software Builds Plans — Not Communications
eMoney and MoneyGuidePro create financial plans with projections and scenarios. But they don't generate the **follow-up email** explaining the plan in plain language, the **quarterly review summary** for the client, or the **estate planning discussion guide** for the meeting.

### 3. Compliance Tools Monitor — They Don't Create
SMARSH archives emails. RegEd tracks supervision. But neither helps advisors **write** compliance-aware communications in the first place. Templates with regulatory guardrails built in are a gap no compliance tool fills.

### 4. Meeting Tools Record — They Don't Prepare
Jump and Zocks transcribe meetings and generate notes. But they don't help advisors **prepare** for the meeting beforehand. And many advisors (especially at wirehouses) refuse recording tools for compliance reasons. The meeting prep gap remains wide open.

| Meeting prep | 5-8 hrs | **YES — biggest opportunity** |
| Post-meeting follow-up | 3-5 hrs | **YES** |
| Email drafting | 3-5 hrs | **YES** |
| CRM documentation | 2-3 hrs | **YES** |

| Prospecting/marketing | 2-4 hrs | **YES** |
| Compliance documentation | 2-3 hrs | Partially |

**Total addressable time: 15-25 hours per week** — the communication and documentation gap between existing systems.

## Implications for Advisor Intelligence

### What This Means for Our Product

1. **Organize around workflow, not planning functions.** Categories should be "Meeting Prep," "Follow-Up & CRM," "Client Emails" — not "Tax Planning," "Retirement Planning," "Estate Planning."

2. **Remove templates that overlap with existing systems.** "Roth Conversion Analysis," "Social Security Optimization," "Retirement Readiness Assessment" signal we don't understand advisor workflows. Cut them.

3. **Reframe educational templates as communication tools.** "Estate Planning Conversation Starter" is valuable — but categorize it as "Meeting Prep," not "Estate Planning." The value is the communication, not the planning.

4. **Build new templates for uncovered gaps.** CRM Note Writer, Action Item Extractor, Quick Market Commentary, Conference Follow-Up — these fill gaps no competitor covers.

5. **Position as the complement, not the replacement.** "Your planning software builds the plan. Your CRM stores the data. Your meeting tool records the call. We handle everything in between."

## The Positioning Statement

> **Old**: "61 templates across 11 categories — AI-powered prompt toolkit for financial advisors"
>
> **New**: "The AI layer for everything your existing systems don't cover — meeting prep, follow-ups, client emails, prospecting, and content creation"

This positioning:
- Acknowledges advisors' existing tech stack (shows we understand their world)
- Identifies a real, unmet need (the communication gap)
- Avoids competing with funded, established tools ($363M+ in recent VC funding for advisor AI tools)
- Positions as a complement that fits into any tech stack
