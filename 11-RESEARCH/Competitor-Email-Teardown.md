# Key Takeaways for Advisor Intelligence

1. **Advisor tech competitors under-invest in email onboarding.** Most wealthtech platforms (Orion, Wealthbox, Altruist) rely on high-touch sales-assisted onboarding with dedicated specialists, not automated email sequences. This is a significant opportunity for Advisor Intelligence to differentiate with a well-crafted, product-led email sequence.
2. **The best SaaS onboarding emails come from outside fintech.** Companies like Linear, Zapier, Loom, and Basecamp set the standard with clean, benefit-focused emails that drive specific actions. Advisor Intelligence should model our emails on these PLG companies, not on enterprise wealthtech.
3. **Snappy Kraken is the closest email-savvy competitor** in the advisor space. They offer pre-built, compliant email campaigns for advisors and recently launched an AI-powered email builder. However, they sell email marketing TO advisors — they don't use great email marketing FOR their own onboarding.
4. **Personal founder touch works.** The most effective welcome emails (Linear, Dub.co, Arcade, Loom) come from a named founder, not a faceless brand. Christopher Baker should sign all onboarding emails personally.
5. **One CTA per email is universal.** Every high-performing SaaS email example reinforces: each email should ask the user to do exactly one thing. Multiple CTAs dilute conversion.

# Advisor Technology Competitors

## Altruist

### Onboarding Approach
- **Type**: Sales-assisted + email-triggered
- **Sequence**: Advisor receives email from `hello@altruist.com` inviting account setup
- **Steps**: (1) Receive invitation email, (2) Create username/password, (3) Log in and follow recommended next steps
- **Dedicated specialist**: Each new advisor gets a personal onboarding contact
- **Support channels**: Phone, chat, and email

### Email Characteristics
- Simple invitation-style email (not a nurture sequence)
- Functional, not marketing-focused
- Drives to account creation, not feature exploration
- No evidence of a multi-email drip sequence for trial users

### What Works
- Clean, direct communication
- Immediate next steps are clear
- Digital-first approach matches their brand (modern custodian)
- Strong support infrastructure behind the emails

### What Doesn't Work
- No evidence of progressive feature education via email
- No behavioral triggers or personalization beyond name
- Relies on high-touch support rather than self-serve onboarding
- Not optimized for product-led growth

### Lesson for Advisor Intelligence
Altruist can afford high-touch onboarding because they're a custodian managing billions in AUM. We're a $49.99/mo SaaS — we need automated, self-serve email onboarding that scales. Our advantage: we can move faster and iterate on email sequences without enterprise bureaucracy.

## Wealthbox CRM

### Onboarding Approach
- **Type**: Sales-assisted + workflow automation
- **Sequence**: Custom automated workflows triggered by events
- **Integration**: Partners with OnBord for client-facing onboarding
- **Email features**: Broadcast email templates, mail merge tags, branded templates

### Email Characteristics
- Workflow-based automation (event triggers create email sequences)
- Email templates with merge fields for personalization
- Focus on advisor-to-client emails, not Wealthbox-to-advisor onboarding
- Integration with OnBord sends welcome emails and texts to clients

### What Works
- Sophisticated workflow automation engine
- Email tied to CRM events (new account, task completion)
- Brandable templates for advisors to use with their clients
- Multi-channel: email + text messaging via OnBord integration

### What Doesn't Work
- Wealthbox's OWN onboarding emails to new users are unremarkable
- Focus is on building tools FOR advisors to email, not emailing advisors themselves
- No public evidence of a trial conversion email sequence
- Heavy reliance on integration partners for onboarding

### Lesson for Advisor Intelligence
Wealthbox shows that advisor tech companies think about email as a feature they build (for advisors to send), not as a growth channel they use (to onboard and retain advisors). This is our opportunity: use email as a first-class growth channel, not just a notification system.

## Orion Advisor Solutions

### Onboarding Approach
- **Type**: High-touch, enterprise sales-assisted
- **Sequence**: Structured multi-week onboarding with dedicated team
- **Timeline**: 14-day initial onboarding + 45, 60, and 90-day training sessions
- **Communication**: In-platform alerts + optional email subscriptions
- **Support**: Dedicated support team, training videos, advisor portal

### Email Characteristics
- Primarily transactional/notification emails
- "Important updates" via in-platform alerts with optional email
- Training session reminders and follow-ups
- No evidence of automated trial conversion sequences (enterprise model)

### What Works
- Structured, time-boxed onboarding (14 days mirrors our 7-day trial)
- Progressive training milestones (Day 45, 60, 90)
- Unified platform experience (Summit Experience)
- Comprehensive support infrastructure

### What Doesn't Work
- Enterprise model doesn't translate to self-serve SaaS
- Email is an afterthought, not a growth channel
- No public evidence of conversion-focused email sequences
- Heavily reliant on human support, not scalable for a startup

### Lesson for Advisor Intelligence
Orion's progressive milestone approach (Day 14, 45, 60, 90) is worth modeling in email. After our 7-day trial, we should have milestone check-ins at Day 14 (1 week as subscriber), Day 30 (1 month), and Day 60. The content shifts from "learn the product" to "get more value" to "you're a power user."

## Holistiplan

### Onboarding Approach
- **Type**: Product-led with support resources
- **Sequence**: No detailed public information about email onboarding
- **Features**: Automated client emails, intake forms, meeting notes with tax/estate insights
- **Partnership**: Works with Nitrogen (formerly Riskalyze) for special pricing bundles

### Email Characteristics
- Holistiplan itself can automate client-facing emails for advisors
- No public evidence of a structured onboarding email sequence for new Holistiplan users
- Support resources and articles available on website
- Integration with Schwab and other platforms for distribution

### What Works
- Strong product that speaks for itself
- Extensive help documentation and resources
- Strategic partnerships (Nitrogen, Schwab) for distribution
- Focus on solving a specific pain point (tax planning)

### What Doesn't Work
- Onboarding appears to be self-serve without guided email nurture
- No visible trial conversion sequence
- Relies on product stickiness rather than email engagement

### Lesson for Advisor Intelligence
Holistiplan succeeds because they solve a very specific, painful problem (tax return analysis). Our emails should focus on specific pain points, not general "AI is great" messaging. Each email should highlight one specific advisor problem and show how our templates solve it.

## Nitrogen (formerly Riskalyze)

### Onboarding Approach
- **Type**: Sales-assisted with product demos
- **Sequence**: No detailed public information about automated email onboarding
- **Focus**: Risk assessment and client engagement software
- **Distribution**: Direct sales + integrations + partnerships

### Email Characteristics
- Marketing emails focus on thought leadership (risk assessment, client engagement)
- Product emails likely include risk report notifications and compliance alerts
- No public evidence of a detailed trial conversion email sequence
- Heavy reliance on sales demos and relationship-driven onboarding

### What Works
- Strong brand positioning ("Fearless Investing")
- Community events (Fearless Investing Summit)
- Content marketing that establishes expertise

### What Doesn't Work
- Enterprise sales model doesn't showcase email onboarding innovation
- No visible self-serve trial flow to analyze

### Lesson for Advisor Intelligence
Nitrogen's strength is its community and events. While we can't compete on events yet, we can create community through email — sharing what other advisors are doing with AI, featuring use cases, and building a sense of belonging among early adopters.

## Snappy Kraken

### Onboarding Approach
- **Type**: Personal onboarding specialist + product setup
- **Setup time**: 3-4 hours (tech-savvy advisors: under 1 hour)
- **Ongoing**: 15 minutes/week for platform maintenance
- **Support**: Dedicated onboarding specialist assigned to each new member

### Email Capabilities (As Product)
- Pre-built, compliant content strategies and campaign flows
- AI-powered email builder for creating campaigns
- Automated nurture and referral email flows
- Content library with compliance-reviewed materials
- Integration with advisor CRM systems

### What Works
- Short setup time (3-4 hours) sets good expectations
- Personal onboarding specialist creates high-touch feel
- Product itself is an email marketing platform, so they understand email deeply
- Compliance-reviewed content gives advisors confidence
- AI-powered email builder is a differentiator

### What Doesn't Work
- Still relies on human specialist for onboarding (doesn't scale cheaply)
- Product complexity requires setup time that may deter some users
- Comparison pages suggest they compete on features, not on experience
- No evidence that their OWN onboarding emails to new users are exceptional

### Lesson for Advisor Intelligence
Snappy Kraken is the most email-savvy company in the advisor tech space, but they sell email tools TO advisors. We can learn from their approach: compliance-aware content, pre-built flows, and AI-assisted creation. But our product is fundamentally simpler — copy-paste templates, not a full marketing automation platform. Our emails should reflect that simplicity.

## FMG Suite

### Onboarding Approach
- **Type**: Content management + website platform
- **Model**: Subscription-based, content-distribution focused
- **Library**: Large library of articles and emails that firms publish under their brand

### Email Characteristics
- Content distribution model (templates that advisors send to their clients)
- Website + content management, not a standalone email platform
- Focus on providing content for advisors to distribute, not on their own email marketing
- "Early era" advisor digital marketing — more static, less data-driven

### What Works
- Extensive content library reduces advisor workload
- Established brand in the advisor marketing space
- Compliance-reviewed content

### What Doesn't Work
- Content-distribution model (not connected/data-driven)
- Less innovation in email automation
- Dated approach compared to modern PLG companies

### Lesson for Advisor Intelligence
FMG represents the "old model" of advisor email marketing — publish pre-written content under your brand. Our model is more innovative: give advisors AI prompt templates they use with Copilot/ChatGPT to create personalized content. Our emails should highlight this differentiation: we're not giving you pre-written emails, we're giving you the AI prompts to create your own.

# Best-in-Class SaaS Email Examples (Outside Advisor Tech)

These companies set the standard for onboarding email excellence. Advisor Intelligence should model our sequences on these examples.

## Linear
- **Subject**: "Welcome to Linear"
- **Key elements**: Core value prop ("software that feels magical"), three essential resources, multiple help paths
- **What makes it great**: Treats email as product experience; design matches product aesthetic; unique CTAs
- **CTA strategy**: Multiple paths — Chrome extension, Showcase, product demo
- **Founder**: CEO signature creates personal connection
- **Takeaway for AI**: Match our email design to our product design. Blue primary color, clean typography, minimal imagery.

## Zapier
- **Subject**: "Welcome to Zapier!"
- **Key elements**: Tangible automation examples, video option, progressive feature introduction
- **What makes it great**: Multiple "Create your first Zap" CTAs driving focused action
- **CTA strategy**: Single core action repeated, with progressive complexity
- **Takeaway for AI**: Our welcome email should drive one action: "Copy Your First Template." Repeat this CTA 2-3 times in different contexts.

## Loom
- **Subject**: "A special welcome to {{first}}"
- **Key elements**: CEO video message demonstrating core value, user proof point, multi-platform options
- **What makes it great**: Dogfoods own product (sends a Loom video in a Loom welcome email)
- **CTA strategy**: Watch video > try product > download apps
- **Founder**: CEO personally welcomes you
- **Takeaway for AI**: We can't dogfood as elegantly, but we can include a template preview directly in the welcome email — show the actual prompt text they'll copy.

## Basecamp
- **Subject**: "Welcome to Basecamp 3!"
- **Key elements**: Positioning against "email, chat, or meetings" chaos, pure utility links
- **What makes it great**: "Sometimes less is more" — no feature upselling, only helpful resources
- **CTA strategy**: Multiple utility links, no sales pressure
- **Takeaway for AI**: Our Day 1 email should be pure utility. No upselling, no "subscribe now." Just help the user succeed with their first template.

## Dub.co
- **Subject**: "Welcome to Dub.co"
- **Key elements**: Founder personal intro, "Short Links With Superpowers" positioning
- **What makes it great**: Three-step action plan for different user types
- **CTA strategy**: Progressive action plan (workspace > link > API)
- **Founder**: Founder-led, personal tone
- **Takeaway for AI**: Create a three-step plan in our welcome email: (1) Complete your profile, (2) Browse templates, (3) Copy and paste into Copilot.

## GitHub
- **Subject**: "Welcome to GitHub!"
- **Key elements**: Celebratory Octocat, concise repository explanation, single clear CTA
- **What makes it great**: Sets welcoming tone while teaching core concepts without overwhelming
- **CTA strategy**: One primary action + gentler alternative (Hello World guide)
- **Takeaway for AI**: Offer one primary action ("Open Your Dashboard") and one gentler alternative ("Watch a 2-minute walkthrough") for users who aren't ready to dive in.

# Synthesis: What Advisor Intelligence Should Build

## Our Advantages Over Advisor Tech Competitors
1. **Simplicity**: Copy-paste model is easier to explain in email than CRM, custodial, or planning software
2. **Speed to value**: Users can get value in 60 seconds (copy a template), not 3-4 hours of setup
3. **No integration required**: No API keys, no CRM setup, no data migration
4. **Founder-led**: Christopher Baker can sign every email — personal touch that enterprise competitors can't match
5. **Product-led growth**: We can use email as a growth channel, not just a support channel

## Email Sequence Priority Order
1. **Build the 7-day trial sequence first** — this directly impacts revenue
2. **Build trial-expired win-back** (Day 7, 14, 30) — recovers lost revenue
3. **Build churn prevention** (7, 14, 21, 30 days inactive) — protects existing revenue
4. **Build post-conversion nurture** (weekly for month 1, biweekly after) — reduces churn
5. **Build newsletter** (biweekly) — builds brand and community

## Design Principles (Learned from Competitors)
- Clean, single-column layout matching our product aesthetic
- Blue primary color (#3152f4) for CTAs
- Plus Jakarta Sans font for brand consistency
- Minimal images — text-first approach
- One CTA per email, repeated 2-3 times
- Founder signature on all onboarding emails
- Mobile-first design (14px+ font, 44x44px buttons)
- Plain-text style for transactional emails
- Branded template for newsletters

## What None of Our Competitors Do (Our Opportunity)
- **Behavior-triggered emails** — "You copied your first template! Here are 3 more like it"
- **Category-specific onboarding** — "You explored Prospecting templates. Here are the top 3 in that category"
- **AI output previews** — Show what the template produces when pasted into Copilot
- **Usage milestones** — "You've copied 10 templates! Here's a Pro tip..."
- **Template of the week** — Weekly email highlighting one underused template with a use case
