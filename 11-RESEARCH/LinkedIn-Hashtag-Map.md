# Key Takeaways

1. **LinkedIn is de-emphasizing hashtags in 2026.** The platform removed hashtag following, eliminated hashtags from profile displays, and stopped showing them in dropdown search. LinkedIn now scans post copy for keywords (SEO-style) rather than relying on hashtags for distribution. Use 1-3 per post maximum.

2. **Niche hashtags outperform broad ones every time.** #AdvisorTech (niche, targeted) delivers higher-quality engagement than #Finance (too broad, too crowded). The goal is discoverability by the right audience, not maximum reach to irrelevant viewers.

3. **Excessive hashtags reduce reach by 68%.** Using more than 5 hashtags correlates with significantly reduced distribution. The 2026 best practice is 1-3 highly relevant tags placed at the end of the post. Never make every word a hashtag.

4. **Branded hashtags only work with consistent, long-term use.** Creating #AdvisorIntelligence will not generate traction unless multiple team members and advocates use it consistently over months. Always pair with established industry hashtags.

5. **Keywords in post body matter more than hashtags now.** Naturally weave target phrases ("financial advisor AI," "compliance-aware prompts," "advisor toolkit") into post text. This is now the primary discovery mechanism.

# Hashtag Strategy Framework

## The 1-3 Rule
- Use **1-3 hashtags per post** (never more than 5)
- Place at the **end of the post** or naturally within text
- Mix **1 broad + 1-2 niche** per post
- Rotate hashtags across posts to avoid repetition penalties
