# Advisor Language Bank --- Voice of Customer Swipe File

## Key Takeaways for Advisor Intelligence

1. **Use "time" and "hours" language constantly** --- advisors think in hours, not dollars. "Save 5 hours a week" lands harder than "increase productivity." Every piece of content should quantify the time savings.
2. **Mirror the frustration-then-relief pattern** --- advisors describe their problems as feeling "out of control," "buried," and "trapped." Then they describe solutions as "game changers" that let them "be present" and "focus on what matters." Our copy should follow this arc.
3. **"Compliance-aware" is the magic phrase** --- advisors are terrified of compliance missteps but tired of compliance theater. They want tools that handle compliance quietly in the background, not ones that lecture them about it.
4. **"No data uploaded" is a differentiator, not a feature** --- in a world where every AI tool wants to ingest client data, the fact that Advisor Intelligence's templates keep client data local is a selling point that should be front and center in every piece of content.
5. **Advisors see themselves as "relationship builders" first** --- they use language like "present with clients," "deepen relationships," "trusted advisor," and "human connection." Our messaging should always reinforce that AI makes them MORE human, not less.

## Phrase Patterns for Copywriting

### The "Before/After" Pattern
Use these pairs in marketing copy:

- **Before:** "4-6 hours to prepare" / **After:** "closer to an hour"
- **Before:** "40 minutes typing notes" / **After:** "95% done in 3-4 minutes"
- **Before:** "an hour transcribing" / **After:** "cut down to 10 minutes"
- **Before:** "buried in compliance documents" / **After:** "be present and engaged"
- **Before:** "chasing paperwork" / **After:** "deepening client relationships"
- **Before:** "out of control of their time" / **After:** "saves considerable time"

### The "Not This / But This" Pattern
Position Advisor Intelligence against complexity:

- **Not** "onboard seven different platforms" / **But** "copy, paste, done"
- **Not** "chasing shiny objects" / **But** "solve the problem you have today"
- **Not** "become a technology expert" / **But** "if you can copy-paste, you can use it"
- **Not** "upload client data to AI" / **But** "your data stays with you"
- **Not** "replacing your judgment" / **But** "enhancing your voice"

### Emotional Trigger Words
Words that resonate with advisor audiences:

**Frustration triggers:** buried, trapped, endless, overwhelming, frustrating, chasing, wasted, duplicating, juggling, scrambling
**Relief triggers:** reclaim, streamline, present, focused, confident, consistent, simple, instant, compliant, polished
**Identity triggers:** trusted advisor, relationship builder, fiduciary, expert, professional, strategic, client-first
**Fear triggers:** liability, exposure, risk, breach, non-compliant, audit, mistake, error

## Usage Instructions for Marketing Agents

When writing ANY content for Advisor Intelligence (LinkedIn posts, blog articles, emails, landing page copy):

1. **Open this file first** and scan for relevant phrases
2. **Use at least 2-3 direct quotes** or paraphrased concepts from this bank
3. **Mirror the emotional arc:** frustration (their current state) -> relief (what the tool does) -> aspiration (who they become)
4. **Quantify time savings** whenever possible --- use hours, not percentages
5. **Never fabricate quotes or statistics** --- only use what's documented here with sources
6. **Match the advisor's self-image:** they see themselves as trusted relationship builders who happen to need better tools, not as technologically incompetent people who need to "get with the times"
