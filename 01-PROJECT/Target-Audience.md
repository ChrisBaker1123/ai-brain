## Primary Users: Financial Advisors

### Firm Types
| Type | Description | AI Restrictions |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Independent RIAs** | Registered Investment Advisors, fiduciary standard, SEC/state regulated | Most flexible โ€” no mandatory pre-approval for AI content |
| **Wirehouses** | Large firms (Merrill, Morgan Stanley, UBS, Wells) | Strictest โ€” FINRA 2210 pre-approval required, conservative language only |
| **Broker-Dealers (IBDs)** | Independent broker-dealers (LPL, Raymond James, Cetera) | FINRA 2210 for retail comms, balanced language |
| **Hybrid RIA/BD** | Dual-registered firms | Apply MORE restrictive standard |
| **Solo Practitioners** | One-person advisory shops | Resource-constrained, most time savings |
| **Family Offices** | Multi-generational wealth management | Complex needs, high AUM |
| **Multi-Advisor Teams** | Ensemble practices | Need consistency across advisors |

### Pain Points (Why They Buy)
1. **Time on admin** โ€” Spending 5+ hours/week writing emails, meeting prep, follow-ups
2. **Email drafting** โ€” Every client email takes 15-25 minutes from scratch
3. **Meeting prep** โ€” Gathering context, building agendas, pre-meeting research
4. **Keeping up with AI** โ€” Know they should use it, don't know how to start
5. **Compliance fears** โ€” Terrified of saying the wrong thing to regulators
6. **Generic AI output** โ€” ChatGPT gives generic, non-personalized content
7. **Consistency** โ€” Different quality every time they prompt AI tools
8. **Client education** โ€” Explaining complex concepts in accessible language

### What They Value
- **Trust** โ€” Won't use tools that feel sketchy or unproven
- **Credibility** โ€” Want to see finance-specific knowledge, not generic SaaS
- **ROI** โ€” Need to see time savings in concrete terms (hours/week)
- **Ease of use** โ€” Zero learning curve, copy-paste model
- **Compliance safety** โ€” Regulatory references give confidence
- **Professional tone** โ€” Output must sound like them, not a robot

### How They Find Tools
- Word of mouth (advisor communities, study groups)
- LinkedIn (primary social platform for advisors)
- Industry conferences (T3, Schwab IMPACT, Riskalyze)
- Custodian ecosystems (Schwab, Fidelity, Pershing)
- Advisor-focused media (Kitces, RIA Intel, InvestmentNews)
