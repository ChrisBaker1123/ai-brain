# RIA Industry Intelligence — Market & Technology Trends

> Last updated: 2026-03-13

## Key Takeaways for Advisor Intelligence

1. **The RIA market is massive and accelerating** — 26,669 SEC-registered firms managing $130T+ in assets, with RIAs projected to manage 33% of all advisor-managed assets by 2026. This is our TAM.
2. **AI adoption has doubled but remains shallow** — 63% of RIAs now use AI (up from ~30% in 2023), but only 1 in 10 are fully integrating it. Most use generative AI for admin tasks (notetaking, email drafting). This is our exact sweet spot.
3. **Fee compression is driving tech adoption** — 83% of advisors expect to charge <1% for $5M+ clients by 2026. Advisors need efficiency tools to maintain margins while expanding services. Advisor Intelligence helps them do more with less.
4. **Record M&A activity creates disruption** — 466 deals in 2025 (27% increase YoY), with PE firms involved in 75.8% of transactions. Breakaway advisors and newly acquired firms both need to upgrade their tech stacks.
5. **70,000 new staff needed in 5 years** — The talent shortage means firms need AI tools to augment existing staff rather than rely on hiring.

| Metric | Value |

| Advisors charging <1% for $5M+ clients (2026) | 83% |
| Average fee for $10M+ clients | ~66 basis points |
| Average fee for $100K clients | ~125 basis points |
| Alternative fee models growing | Flat rates, hourly billing, subscription fees |

**Impact on Advisor Intelligence:** Fee compression means advisors need to:
- Serve more clients with same staff (AI tools enable this)
- Expand service offerings without proportional cost increases
- Differentiate through expertise and personalization, not price
- Adopt alternative fee models that require better client communication templates

## Breakaway & Independence Trends

Sources: [Wealth Management](https://www.wealthmanagement.com/ria-news/ria-leaders-wirehouse-breakaways-expanded-services-more-m-a-to-dominate-26), [AdvizorPro](https://advizorpro.com/post/advisor-movement-trends-report-2025)

- Pipeline of wirehouse-to-RIA transitions remains robust entering 2026
- 511 new RIAs launched in 2025 with $80.6B in AUM (more than double 2024 levels)
- Most breakaway advisors opt for hybrid platforms initially (flexibility + compliance support)
- PE-backed platforms offering turnkey infrastructure have lowered barriers to independence
- Wirehouses losing fewer advisors than previous years (improved retention strategies)
- Successful independent advisor in 2026 builds "networked firms" — replacing overhead with strategic partnerships
- Key challenge for new independents: replacing wirehouse research, analyst coverage, and data infrastructure

**Advisor Intelligence opportunity:** Breakaway advisors need to quickly build capabilities they had at wirehouses. Our prompt templates help them generate client-quality communications, research summaries, and planning content without dedicated support staff.

## PE / M&A Activity

Sources: [PLANADVISER](https://www.planadviser.com/ria-ma-breaks-records-in-2025/), [InvestmentNews](https://www.investmentnews.com/ria-news/ria-ma-poised-for-another-record-year-in-2026-devoe-finds/263686), [Wealth Management](https://www.wealthmanagement.com/ria-news/2025-in-review-private-equity-drives-ria-m-a)

### 2025 Record-Breaking Year
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total RIA M&A deals | 466 (27% increase over 2024) |
| Financial sponsors involved | 353 deals (75.8% of total) |
| Acquired assets | ~$2.5 trillion |
| Largest transaction | Bain Capital investment in Lincoln Financial ($321B) |
| Most active acquirer | Carson Group (20+ acquisitions) |
| PE ownership of industry assets | ~25% (despite only 3.7% of firms) |

### 2026 Outlook
- Another record year expected (Echelon Partners, DeVoe & Company)
- Creative PE structures emerging: minority stakes, multi-sponsor capital tables, recapitalizations
- Mid-market RIAs ($1B-$10B) beginning to pair up for defensive positioning
- PE interest widening to smaller RIAs with strong growth rates and next-gen leadership
- Focus on scaled wealth platforms with embedded distribution for alternative products
- PE-driven culture concerns growing: [Financial Planning reports](https://www.financial-planning.com/opinion/how-private-equity-acquirers-are-gutting-ria-culture) PE money can eviscerate client-first culture

## Regulatory & Compliance Technology

Sources: [SEC 2026 Exam Priorities](https://www.goodwinlaw.com/en/insights/publications/2025/12/alerts-privateequity-pif-2026-sec-exam-priorities-for-registered-investment-advisers), [FINRA 2026 Oversight Report](https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2025/12/finra-issues-2026-regulatory-oversight-report)

### SEC 2026 Exam Priorities
- **AI & Technology focus:** Reviewing firm claims about AI capabilities for accuracy; assessing policies/procedures to supervise AI use
- **Cybersecurity:** Shifting from broad checks to detailed governance and operational integrity audits
- **Fiduciary duty:** Continued emphasis on compliance programs and suitability

### FINRA 2026 Oversight Report
- Firms must assess regulatory compliance obligations **before** deploying GenAI
- Establish governance frameworks to supervise GenAI usage
- Key areas: Reg BI compliance, third-party vendor management, best execution

### AML Requirements
- FinCEN's AML/CFT obligations for investment advisers deadline pushed to January 1, 2028 (from original January 1, 2026)

**Advisor Intelligence angle:** Our compliance-aware prompts help advisors stay within regulatory guardrails when using AI. The SEC specifically examines AI claims and supervision — our templates include built-in compliance guardrails referencing SEC/FINRA rules. This is a differentiator vs. generic AI tools.
