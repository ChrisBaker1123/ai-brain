# Key Takeaways for Advisor Intelligence
1. Conquest's "compliance-first AI" positioning is unique and something we should watch — they're building AI features that satisfy regulators from day one, not retrofitting compliance later. Our SEC/FINRA guardrails in every template mirror this philosophy.
2. Despite 60% of Canada's advice market, Conquest has failed to gain significant US traction against eMoney/RightCapital/MoneyGuide. Being Canadian-first may limit their US relevance for now.
3. Conquest's SAM (Strategic Advice Manager) recommends the "next best financial decision" — this is the closest any competitor comes to our template model (suggesting specific actions), but it operates within a full planning platform, not as standalone templates.

# Conquest Planning

## Overview
Conquest Planning is a Canadian fintech founded in 2018 by Mark Evans (who also founded NaviPlan, one of the first financial planning platforms). Based in Winnipeg, Manitoba, with offices in UK and USA. Their Strategic Advice Manager (SAM) uses AI algorithms to process client data and recommend the "next best financial decision." Claims to represent 60% of Canada's financial advice market. Raised $80M Series B in 2025.

## Pricing
- **Unlimited Plan**: $2,400/year or $200/month per advisor (unlimited plans)
- **Pay-Per-Plan**: $45 per plan (one investment plan per household)
- **Enterprise**: $25-$50 per plan for large enterprise clients, depending on complexity
- Plan packages are non-refundable in the year purchased
- Once a plan is created, unlimited use of all tools (scenarios, sandboxes, revisions) for that household for the year
- Distribution through partners: Investia, IDC, Pershing X/Wove, Fidelity (UK)

## Target Market & Firm Size Sweet Spot
- **Primary**: Canadian financial advisory firms (dominant market position)
- **Secondary**: US market via Pershing X/Wove partnership (limited traction so far)
- **UK expansion**: Growing presence
- Large dealer networks and bank-affiliated advisory channels
- Firms wanting "guided" financial planning (not just calculators)

## Product Features

### Core Platform
- Strategic Advice Manager (SAM): AI that recommends "next best financial decision"
- Comprehensive financial planning (retirement, tax, estate, insurance)
- Goal-based planning with probability modeling

### April 2026 AI Capabilities (Announced)
- **SAM Guide**: Natural language Q&A about plan navigation, KPIs, reporting, strategy context
- **SAM Bytes**: Guided analysis of complex multi-strategy scenarios (first: "Where to Save?" covering tax-deferred, tax-free, taxable account strategies; upcoming: debt management, home buying, pension, decumulation)
- **LLM Data Migration**: AI ingests unstructured data from PDFs, Word, Excel, CSVs and pushes into client plans
- **MCP (Model Context Protocol)**: Enables LLMs and AI agents to access Conquest data
- Compliance-first AI design: all features built with regulatory requirements embedded

## Strengths (what they do better than advisorintelligence.app)
- Deep financial planning AI that produces actual plan recommendations (not templates)
- Compliance-first approach to AI embedded in every feature
- Founder credibility (NaviPlan pedigree)
- MCP integration allows LLMs to access planning data — forward-thinking architecture
- Strong Canadian market position and institutional partnerships
- Significant VC backing ($100M+ total raised)

## Weaknesses & Gaps (what advisors complain about)
- **US market failure**: Despite being available since 2022, negligible US market share
- **Complexity**: "New users will get frustrated with its complexity but be surprised by Conquest's capability once they learn how to use it" (advisor feedback)
- **Non-refundable packages**: Once committed, advisors are locked in regardless of usage
- **Canadian-centric**: Many features, tax calculations, and regulatory assumptions built for Canada first
- **No content creation**: SAM recommends planning strategies but doesn't help advisors write emails, create marketing, or prospect
- **Limited US content strategy**: No blog, educational resources, or community building for US market
- **Enterprise-only distribution**: No self-serve signup for individual advisors in the US

## How Advisor Intelligence Differentiates
1. **US-first, self-serve** — Conquest sells through enterprise partnerships (Pershing X). We're direct-to-advisor with immediate signup at $49.99/month.
2. **Content creation vs. plan creation** — Conquest creates financial plans. We help advisors create content, communications, and marketing materials around those plans.
3. **Zero complexity** — Conquest's learning curve frustrates new users. Our copy-paste templates require no training.
4. **Platform-agnostic** — Conquest requires buying into their planning platform. Our templates work with any planning software.
5. **Complementary** — Advisors using Conquest for financial plans still need help communicating those plans to clients, prospecting new clients, and creating marketing content.
