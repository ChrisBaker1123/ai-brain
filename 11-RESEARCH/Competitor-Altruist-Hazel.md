# Key Takeaways for Advisor Intelligence
1. Altruist's Hazel at $60/seat/month (base) to $125/seat/month (with tax planning) is a serious threat because it bundles AI across meetings, emails, documents, CRM data, AND custodial data into one platform. No other tool has this data breadth.
2. Altruist's content marketing playbook (17-page content strategy guide for advisors, blog.altruist.com/marketing) is the gold standard for advisor-facing content — we should study their SEO structure, content categories, and editorial voice.
3. Hazel's launch triggered a STOCK SELL-OFF of Schwab, LPL, and Raymond James — Wall Street sees custodian-embedded AI as an existential threat to traditional firms. This validates the AI-for-advisors market opportunity massively.

# Altruist / Hazel

## Overview
Altruist is a modern RIA custodian founded by Jason Wenk, built to challenge Schwab and Fidelity with lower costs and better technology. Hazel is their AI platform, launched September 2025, that unifies firm data (conversations, emails, documents, CRM, calendars) with market, regulatory, and custodial insights. In February 2026, Hazel added AI-powered tax planning. Altruist has raised $152M in Series F funding.

## The Operating Layer Strategy
Altruist's thesis: **the RIA custodian will become the advisor's operating system**. By bundling custody, account opening, trading, portfolio management, billing, reporting, AND AI into one platform, they eliminate the need for advisors to license 5-10 separate tools. This "operating layer" approach is unique — no other custodian offers embedded AI at this level.

Key strategic advantage: **Altruist doesn't compete with its own clients.** Unlike Schwab (which runs its own direct-to-consumer advisory) and Fidelity (same), Altruist only serves advisors. This creates trust.

## Pricing
- **Hazel Base (Admin AI)**: $60/seat/month or $600/seat/year
- **Hazel Paraplanner Lite** (no meeting joining): ~$250-$300/year for admin seats
- **Hazel with Tax Planning**: $125/seat/month
- **Custodial services**: Free (Altruist makes money on cash spreads and securities lending, like all custodians)
- **Hazel available to non-Altruist firms**: You don't need to custody with Altruist to use Hazel

## Target Market & Firm Size Sweet Spot
- **Primary**: High-growth RIAs (firms adding clients rapidly)
- **Secondary**: Established RIAs looking to modernize their tech stack
- **Enterprise**: Large RIAs with 50+ advisors needing unified operations
- **Sweet spot**: Growth-stage firms with $100M-$1B AUM that want modern tech without legacy custodian baggage
- Firms with 1-50 advisors who want all-in-one simplicity

## Product Features

### Hazel AI Platform
- **Ask Hazel**: Natural language Q&A across all connected firm data (transcripts, emails, CRM, custodial data)
- **Meeting Capture**: Records and summarizes client discussions
- **Follow-up Drafting**: Auto-generates post-meeting emails
- **Task Prioritization**: AI-powered to-do management
- **Anticipatory Intelligence**: Predicts what the advisor needs next

### Hazel Tax Planning (Feb 2026)
- Reads 1040s, paystubs, account statements, meeting notes, emails, custodial data, CRM data
- Applies deep tax logic to create personalized tax strategies
- Interactive scenario modeling ("what-if" questions: bonus impact, home sale, retirement transition)
- Real-time projected tax outcome updates
- Zero data retention with AI model providers (data not used for training)

### Altruist Custodial Platform
- Digital account opening (minutes, not days)
- Automated rebalancing (robo-like efficiency)
- Performance reporting
- Billing
- Trading
- All integrated into one platform

## Strengths (what they do better than advisorintelligence.app)
- **Data breadth**: Hazel accesses meeting transcripts, emails, CRM, custodial data, and documents — no other AI tool has this unified view
- **Custodial integration**: Tax planning that reads actual account balances, rates of return, and allocations is impossible to replicate without custodial access
- **VC backing**: $152M in funding vs. our bootstrapped operation
- **Content marketing**: Their advisor marketing blog is genuinely best-in-class, with actionable playbooks
- **Brand credibility**: Fast Company recognition, 573 employees, industry figure founder
- **Platform lock-in potential**: Once advisors custody AND use Hazel, switching costs are enormous

## Weaknesses & Gaps (what advisors complain about)
- **AI reliability concerns**: "Tax planning failures can occur in silent, tail-risky, and liability-shaped ways" — their own disclosure acknowledges hallucination risk
- **Audit trail questions**: Regulators may struggle to verify why Hazel produced a given output — reproducibility is an open question
- **Prompt injection risk**: Ingested PDFs and emails are attack surfaces for AI manipulation
- **Zero data retention claims may not cover all subprocessors** (OCR pipelines, embedding services, logging infrastructure)
- **New product risk**: Hazel launched Sept 2025, tax planning Feb 2026 — very new, untested at scale
- **Market reaction was dramatic**: Hazel tax planning launch triggered stock sell-offs in Schwab, LPL, Raymond James — but also raised questions about whether AI tax planning is truly ready
- **Custodial lock-in**: Hazel works best with Altruist custody; non-Altruist firms get reduced functionality
- **No content creation templates**: Hazel answers questions and summarizes meetings but doesn't generate marketing content, prospecting emails, or client education materials
- **Price adds up**: $125/seat/month for tax planning = $1,500/year per advisor — expensive for smaller firms

## How Advisor Intelligence Differentiates
1. **Content creation vs. data synthesis** — Hazel synthesizes firm data and answers questions. We provide ready-to-use templates for prospecting, client communication, marketing, and practice management. Different tools for different problems.
2. **Platform-agnostic** — Hazel is tightly coupled to Altruist (and partially to Wealthbox). Our templates work with any AI tool (Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude) at any firm using any custodian.
3. **70% cheaper** — $600/year vs $720-$1,500/year for Hazel. And no custodial relationship required.
4. **Zero data risk** — Hazel ingests tax returns, emails, CRM data, and account statements. We never touch client data. For compliance-conscious firms, this is decisive.
5. **Immediate value** — Hazel requires data connections, CRM integration, and custodial setup. Our templates work the moment you sign up.
6. **Content Altruist doesn't provide** — Despite their excellent marketing blog, Altruist/Hazel doesn't offer templates for advisor-to-client communication, prospecting sequences, or practice management content. That's our entire product.

## Altruist's Content Strategy: What We Should Learn
1. Their 17-page Content Marketing Playbook establishes authority before the sale — we should consider a similar "AI Prompt Playbook for Advisors" free resource
2. Blog posts target exact advisor pain points with specific SEO keywords — their "content marketing for financial advisors" page likely ranks #1 for that query
3. They use educational content to build trust without selling — their blog rarely mentions Altruist products directly
4. Multiple content formats (blog, video, podcast, webinars, downloadable guides) — we currently have tutorials but could expand
5. Employee advocacy on LinkedIn amplifies reach without paid advertising

## What Advisor Intelligence Can Learn from Altruist (Without Copying)
1. **Position as infrastructure, not feature**: "Operating layer" language makes switching costly. We should position our templates as the "communication layer" for advisory practices.
2. **Investor-quality testimonials with full disclaimers**: Altruist includes detailed testimonial disclaimers per SEC requirements — we should adopt similar compliance rigor.
3. **Timeline-based onboarding**: "30 days to live" reduces switching anxiety. We should create "2 minutes to your first template" messaging.
4. **Premium blog content**: Their content marketing playbook is genuinely useful to advisors, building trust before the sale.
5. **Podcast presence**: The Advisor Journey builds community. A short-form podcast or video series about AI prompt best practices could differentiate us.
