# Key Takeaways for Advisor Intelligence
1. Jump claims ~27,000 advisors (nearly 10% of all US financial advisors) and raised $80M Series B — the AI-for-advisors space is attracting serious capital. Our $49.99/mo needs to capture this momentum.
2. Jump is evolving from notetaker to "AI Operating System for Advisors" — if they add content/template features, they become a direct competitor. We should establish our template moat before they expand.
3. VRGL's portfolio analysis is narrow (prospecting/proposals only) but their March 2026 platform expansion into "unified growth infrastructure" signals ambition to be a broader advisor tool.

# Jump

## Overview
Jump is an AI operating system for financial advisors. Initially launched as an AI meeting notetaker, it has expanded into a unified platform with three products: Meet (meeting automation), Grow (scaling best practices), and Operate (intake/email/document automation). Nearly 1 in 10 US financial advisors now use Jump, adding 2,000+ new advisors per month.

## Pricing
- Subscription model: ~$75-$175 per advisor per month (volume-dependent)
- Average estimated cost: ~$120/month per advisor
- Enterprise pricing: custom

## Target Market & Firm Size Sweet Spot
- Independent RIAs of all sizes
- Growth-focused firms adding 2,000+ new advisor users monthly
- Firms wanting to automate meeting workflows and compliance documentation

## Product Features
- **Meet**: Pre/post-meeting task automation, saving 5-15 hours per week
- **Grow**: Analyzes top-performer behaviors, scales best practices across firm
- **Operate**: Automates intake, email, and document processes
- Meeting transcription, notes, CRM updates, and compliance records
- Integrations with financial planning tools, CRMs, calendars
- Holistiplan integration for tax context in meetings

## Messaging & Positioning
- "AI Operating System for Financial Advisors"
- Emphasis on time savings: "5-15 hours per week"
- Growth-oriented language: "transform wealth management workflows"

## Trust Signals & Social Proof
- ~27,000 advisors (nearly 10% of US financial advisors)
- $80M Series B funding (led by Insight Partners, announced Feb 2026)
- Adding 2,000+ new advisors per month
- #1 in advisor satisfaction and adoption (2025 T3/Inside Information Software Survey)
- #1 in the 2025 Kitces Report On Financial Advisor Technology Use
- #1 market leader in AI notetaker category for advisors (Kitces Research)

## LinkedIn Presence
- 251 followers (surprisingly low given user adoption — suggests growth through product/referral, not social)

# VRGL

## Overview
VRGL (pronounced "vergil") is a wealth management technology company focused on streamlining investment proposals and portfolio analysis. Founded in 2021 in Dallas, TX, VRGL uses OCR to extract data from investment account statements and compare prospect portfolios against advisor models across 5 pillars: performance, risk, diversification, taxes, and fees.

## Pricing
- Not publicly disclosed; contact sales
- Enterprise/firm-level pricing

## Target Market & Firm Size Sweet Spot
- Growth-focused RIAs and wirehouses
- Firms focused on prospect conversion and AUM acquisition
- Mid-to-large firms with active business development teams

## Product Features (March 2026 platform expansion)
- **VRGL Core**: Statement extraction, portfolio analytics, risk tolerance questionnaires, proposal generation, tax transition optimization
- **Advanced Capabilities**: Deeper analytics and reporting
- **Optional Modules**: Specialized add-ons
- OCR statement extraction: 75% reduction in time to portfolio analysis
- 5-pillar analysis: performance, risk, diversification, taxes, fees
- 30% increase in conversion rates (claimed)
- Integration with custodians (Schwab)

## Messaging & Positioning
- "Guide clients with quantitative portfolio analysis"
- "Accelerate client acquisition"
- Evolving from "proposal software" to "unified growth platform"

## Trust Signals & Social Proof
- 2024 WealthTech100 selection
- $15M Series A funding (MissionOG + FINTOP Capital)
- Schwab Advisor Services partner
- Listed in Kitces FinTech Directory

## LinkedIn Presence
- 6,592 followers

## Kitces Coverage
- Listed in Kitces FinTech Directory for Proposal Generation
- Featured in Kitces Software Showcase for Proposal Software
- Regular mentions in AdvisorTech monthly roundups
- Kitces describes VRGL as applying "OCR capabilities to investment account statements" for 5-pillar analysis

## Strengths (what they do better than advisorintelligence.app)
- **Jump**: Massive adoption velocity (2,000 new advisors/month), deep meeting workflow integration, real-time compliance documentation
- **VRGL**: Quantitative portfolio analysis with hard data, direct prospect conversion impact (30% lift), institutional-grade analytics

## Weaknesses & Gaps
- **Jump**: Low LinkedIn presence (251 followers) suggests reliance on direct sales; operating system ambition may lead to feature bloat; pricing at $75-$175/mo is 1.5-3.5x our cost
- **Jump app reliability**: App Store reviews reveal recordings disappearing after upload, freezing issues, notifications getting stuck — though recent reviews say "super reliable now"
- **Jump onboarding**: "This is a highly customizable system, so advisors seeking an out-of-the-box solution might find better alternatives elsewhere" — steeper learning curve than expected
- **VRGL**: Narrow use case (prospecting/proposals only); requires uploading client/prospect statements; no public pricing; no content creation capabilities
- Neither product helps advisors write better emails, create marketing content, or communicate with clients

## How Advisor Intelligence Differentiates
1. **Content creation, not workflow automation** — Jump automates meeting workflows; VRGL automates proposals. We help advisors create compelling content, communications, and marketing materials.
2. **No data upload** — Both Jump and VRGL process sensitive client data. Our copy-paste model keeps data with the advisor.
3. **Price advantage** — $49.99/mo vs Jump's $75-175/mo. VRGL's enterprise pricing is likely even higher.
4. **Complementary tools** — Advisors using Jump for meetings and VRGL for proposals still need Advisor Intelligence for everything else: prospecting emails, client education, marketing content.
