# 2026-03-14 — Product Strategy Pivot: Template Audit & Gap-Filler Positioning

## What Was Done

### Phase 1: Deep Research
- Searched 20+ queries via brave-search across 3 parallel research agents
- Analyzed: Schwab RIA & AI Study (2026), T3/Inside Information (2025/2026), Advisor360 (2025/2026), Horsesmouth (2024/2025), Kitces AdvisorTech, LPL Focus, EY GenAI, ACA/NSCP AI Benchmarking, Betterment Advisor Survey
- Key finding confirmed: advisors use AI for communication/admin tasks (meeting notes 43%, emails 43%, content 25%), NOT for financial planning (only 29%), portfolio management, or tax analysis

### Phase 2: Complete Template Audit
- Audited all 61 templates against the "Morgan Stanley advisor test"
- Classification: 30 KEEP, 18 RETHINK, 13 REMOVE
- Key removals: Roth Conversion Analysis, Social Security Optimization, Retirement Readiness Assessment, Tax-Loss Harvesting Review, all 7 of 8 Deep Analysis templates
- Key reframes: Estate planning templates → Meeting Prep; Tax/retirement education → Client Education

### Phase 3: New Category Architecture
- Designed 8 workflow-based categories (replacing 11 planning-function categories)
- Categories: Meeting Prep, Follow-Up & CRM, Client Emails, Client Education, Prospecting & Growth, Content Creation, New Client Onboarding, Practice Operations
- Net result: 61 → 68 templates (13 removed, 19 new proposed)

### Phase 4: Competitive Analysis
- Researched Jump AI, Zocks, Pulse360, FP Alpha, and prompt libraries
- Confirmed: NO competitor organizes by advisor workflow; all organize by planning function or meeting stage
- Confirmed: communication/content gap is wide open — no funded competitor fills it

### Phase 5: Strategy Document
- Wrote comprehensive [[Product-Strategy-Pivot]] with all classifications, new architecture, implementation plan

### Phase 6: Verification
- Triple-verified template classifications against research data
- Confirmed positioning resonates for both wirehouse and independent RIA advisors
- Validated new demo flow narrative
