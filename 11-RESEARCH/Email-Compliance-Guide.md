# Key Takeaways for Advisor Intelligence

1. **Advisor Intelligence is a technology vendor, NOT an investment adviser.** Our emails market software, not investment advice. This means SEC Marketing Rule (206(4)-1) does NOT directly apply to our emails. However, we should still follow its spirit because our customers (advisors) are subject to these rules, and our credibility depends on regulatory awareness.
2. **CAN-SPAM is the primary regulation for our emails.** Every commercial email must include: accurate sender info, honest subject lines, identification as an ad (if applicable), a valid physical address, and a working unsubscribe mechanism. Penalties: up to $51,744 per non-compliant email.
3. **Never include performance claims, testimonials, or guarantees** in our emails. Even though we're not regulated as an adviser, making claims like "advisors who use our templates see 3x more client engagement" would create compliance issues for advisors who forward our emails and expose us to FTC scrutiny.
4. **Transactional emails (password resets, subscription confirmations) are exempt** from most CAN-SPAM requirements but should still include physical address for trust.
5. **Archive everything.** While not legally required as a tech vendor, maintaining email records protects the business and builds trust with advisor customers who are required to archive communications. Keep records for at least 3 years.

# SEC Marketing Rule (Rule 206(4)-1)

## How It Applies to Advisor Intelligence

**Direct application**: The SEC Marketing Rule applies to investment advisers registered under the Advisers Act. Advisor Intelligence is NOT a registered investment adviser — we are a software vendor. The rule does not directly regulate our marketing emails.

**Indirect relevance**: Our customers ARE investment advisers. If we make claims about investment performance, provide testimonials about investment outcomes, or position our tool as providing investment advice, we could be seen as aiding non-compliant marketing by our customers. Additionally, advisors may forward our emails to clients or use our copy in their own communications, so our content must be "advisor-safe."

## Key Provisions (for reference)

### General Prohibitions
All advertising must not:
1. Contain untrue statements or omit material facts
2. Make unsubstantiated material claims
3. Create misleading implications
4. Present benefits without corresponding risks/limitations
5. Reference specific investment advice in an unbalanced way
6. Include/exclude performance results unfairly
7. Be otherwise materially misleading

### Testimonial and Endorsement Rules
- **Written agreements** required for compensated promoters
- **Clear disclosures** must accompany all testimonials:
  - Whether the person is a current client
  - Whether compensation was provided
  - Any material conflicts of interest
- **For Advisor Intelligence**: We cannot publish fake testimonials. If we eventually have real user testimonials, we should include disclosures modeled on SEC requirements even though we're not technically required to.

### Performance Advertising Restrictions
- Gross returns must be accompanied by equally prominent net-of-fees returns
- Must show 1-year, 5-year, and 10-year periods with equal prominence
- Hypothetical performance must include assumptions, methodology, risks, and limitations
- **For Advisor Intelligence**: We should NEVER claim our templates produce specific investment returns or performance improvements. Focus on time savings, efficiency, and workflow benefits instead.

### Recordkeeping Requirements
- Advisers must preserve marketing content as actually delivered
- Must retain disclosures, internal approvals, and decision rationale
- 10-year retention for testimonial-related records
- **For Advisor Intelligence**: While not legally required, we should archive our email campaigns in a retrievable format. This builds trust with advisor customers and protects us legally.

## What We CAN Say in Emails
- "Save hours on client communications"
- "61 compliance-aware templates"
- "Built for financial advisors"
- "Copy, paste, and customize for your practice"
- "Compliance guardrails built into every template"

## What We CANNOT Say in Emails
- "Advisors who use our tool get better investment outcomes"
- "Guaranteed to increase AUM"
- "Our AI provides investment advice"
- "#1 tool for financial advisors" (unsubstantiated superlative)
- "Risk-free trial" (avoid "risk-free" language in financial context)
- Any fabricated testimonials or user counts

# FINRA Rule 2210 (Communications with the Public)

## How It Applies to Advisor Intelligence

**Direct application**: FINRA Rule 2210 applies to broker-dealers and their associated persons. Advisor Intelligence is NOT a broker-dealer, so this rule does not directly regulate us.

**Indirect relevance**: Some of our advisor customers may be associated with broker-dealers. If they forward our emails, quote our marketing copy, or use our templates in communications subject to FINRA review, the content needs to be "FINRA-safe."

## Three Categories of Communications

### 1. Retail Communication
- Any written or electronic communication distributed to more than 25 retail investors within 30 calendar days
- **Our marketing emails** would be classified as retail communications if sent by a broker-dealer. Since we're a tech vendor, this doesn't apply directly, but our content should pass retail communication standards.

### 2. Institutional Communication
- Communications distributed exclusively to institutional investors
- Not relevant to our email marketing

### 3. Correspondence
- Written or electronic communication to 25 or fewer retail investors within 30 calendar days
- One-to-one replies to advisor inquiries fall here

## Content Standards (Reference for Our Copy)
All communications must be:
- **Fair and balanced** — present both sides, don't overstate benefits
- **Based on sound basis** — claims must be supportable
- **Not misleading** — no exaggerations, no omissions of material facts
- **Clear about risks** — if discussing anything related to investing

## Filing Requirements
- Certain retail communications must be filed with FINRA within 10 business days of first use
- New member firms must pre-file retail communications for the first year
- **For Advisor Intelligence**: Not applicable to us, but we should be aware that advisors may need to pre-file materials that include our content.

## Supervision Requirements
- Firms must designate a registered principal to review communications before use
- All communications must be maintained per FINRA recordkeeping rules (3 years)
- **For Advisor Intelligence**: If advisors forward our emails, those forwarded emails become the advisor's communications subject to supervision.

## Practical Implications for Our Emails
1. Never position ourselves as providing investment advice
2. Never make performance promises or guarantees
3. Keep language factual and balanced
4. Include appropriate context when discussing AI capabilities
5. Make it clear we're a technology tool, not an advisory service

# CAN-SPAM Requirements for Financial Services

## Overview
The CAN-SPAM Act (Controlling the Assault of Non-Solicited Pornography And Marketing Act of 2003) is the primary federal law governing commercial email in the United States. It applies to ALL commercial emails, regardless of industry.

## Seven Core Requirements

### 1. Accurate Header Information
- "From," "To," and "Reply-To" must accurately identify the sender
- Domain and email address must reflect the sending organization
- **For AI**: Use `Christopher Baker <hello@advisorintelligence.app>` — not a misleading sender name

### 2. Honest Subject Lines
- Must accurately reflect the content of the message
- Cannot be deceptive or misleading
- **For AI**: "Your trial ends tomorrow" is fine. "URGENT: Account compromised" when it's a marketing email is NOT fine.

### 3. Identify the Message as an Ad
- Commercial messages must be identifiable as advertisements
- The law gives flexibility in how to do this
- **For AI**: Transactional emails (welcome, subscription confirmation) don't need this. Marketing emails (newsletter, feature updates) should be clearly commercial in nature.

### 4. Physical Address
- Must include a valid physical postal address in every commercial email
- Options: street address, registered PO Box, or registered private mailbox
- **For AI**: Use the business address or a registered PO Box. Include in email footer.

### 5. Unsubscribe Mechanism
- Every commercial email must include a clear, conspicuous unsubscribe option
- Must be easy to use (single click preferred)
- Cannot require personally identifying information beyond email address
- Cannot charge a fee to unsubscribe
- Opt-out mechanism must work for at least 30 days after sending
- **For AI**: Use Resend's built-in unsubscribe functionality. Include "Unsubscribe" link in footer of every marketing email.

### 6. Honor Opt-Out Requests Promptly
- Must process unsubscribe requests within 10 business days
- Cannot transfer or sell the email address after an opt-out request
- Cannot send any further commercial messages after opt-out (transactional OK)
- **For AI**: Resend handles this automatically when using their unsubscribe feature. Ensure the Supabase `email_preferences` table is updated on unsubscribe.

### 7. Monitor Third-Party Compliance
- If using third-party services for email, you remain responsible for compliance
- Both the brand and the sending service can be held liable
- **For AI**: Using Resend is fine, but we're still responsible for content compliance.

## Penalties
- **Up to $51,744 per non-compliant email** (FTC-enforced)
- Criminal penalties possible for aggravated violations (up to 5 years imprisonment)
- ISPs can seek injunctive relief, actual damages, and statutory damages
- Both the company and individual decision-makers can be held liable

## Transactional vs. Commercial Email

| Characteristic | Transactional | Commercial |
|---------------|--------------|------------|
| Purpose | Facilitate a transaction, provide account info | Promote a product or service |
| Examples | Password reset, subscription confirmation, receipts | Newsletter, feature updates, promotions |
| CAN-SPAM applies? | Limited — header accuracy and no deception | Full requirements apply |
| Unsubscribe required? | No | Yes |
| Physical address required? | Recommended but not required | Required |
| Identify as ad? | No | Yes |

**For AI, transactional emails include**: Welcome/account creation, password reset, subscription confirmation/receipt, trial expiration notice (borderline — include unsubscribe to be safe), payment failure notification.

**For AI, commercial emails include**: Feature highlights, newsletters, re-engagement campaigns, promotional offers, new template announcements.

# Practical Compliance Checklist

## Pre-Send Checklist (Every Email)

### Header & Identification
- [ ] "From" name and email accurately identify Advisor Intelligence
- [ ] "Reply-To" address works and is monitored
- [ ] Subject line accurately reflects email content
- [ ] No deceptive or misleading header information

### Content
- [ ] No unsubstantiated claims about product performance
- [ ] No fake testimonials or fabricated user counts
- [ ] No guarantees, "risk-free" language, or superlatives ("#1," "best")
- [ ] No investment advice or performance promises
- [ ] Content is factual and balanced
- [ ] AI capabilities described accurately (not overstated)
- [ ] Clear that we're a technology vendor, not an advisory firm

### Footer Requirements
- [ ] Valid physical address included
- [ ] Working unsubscribe link (for commercial emails)
- [ ] Unsubscribe mechanism is easy and clear
- [ ] Company name clearly displayed

### Technical
- [ ] SPF/DKIM/DMARC properly configured on sending domain
- [ ] Sending from verified domain (not personal email)
- [ ] Plain-text version included alongside HTML
- [ ] Mobile-responsive design tested
- [ ] All links working and pointing to correct destinations

### Recordkeeping
- [ ] Email content archived with send date
- [ ] Recipient list preserved
- [ ] Opt-out requests logged and honored within 10 business days

## Phrases to AVOID in Financial Services Emails

| Avoid | Why | Use Instead |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| "Guaranteed results" | Misleading; no results can be guaranteed | "Designed to help you..." |
| "Risk-free" | Implies no risk; problematic in financial context | "7-day free trial" |
| "#1 advisor tool" | Unsubstantiated superlative | "Built specifically for advisors" |
| "Proven to increase AUM" | Unsubstantiated performance claim | "Helps streamline client communication" |
| "AI-powered financial advice" | We don't provide advice | "AI-powered prompt templates" |
| "Compliance-approved" | We're not a compliance authority | "Compliance-aware" |
| "Best in class" | Unsubstantiated superlative | "Purpose-built for advisors" |
| "Act now" / "Limited time" (false) | Deceptive if not true | Only use if genuinely time-limited |
| "500+ advisors trust us" | Fabricated social proof | Omit until we have real data |
| "FREE" (all caps) | Spam trigger | "free trial" (lowercase, in context) |

## Archiving & Retention Requirements

### As a Technology Vendor (Our Obligations)
- **CAN-SPAM**: No specific archiving requirement, but maintain records of opt-out requests
- **FTC**: Keep records of commercial email practices for at least 3 years (best practice)
- **State laws**: Some states have additional requirements; maintain compliance with all applicable state laws

### For Our Advisor Customers (Their Obligations, Our Awareness)
- **SEC Rule 17a-4**: Advisers must retain communications for 6+ years
- **FINRA Rule 3110**: Firms must review and archive incoming/outgoing correspondence
- **FINRA Rule 4511**: Records must be preserved for 3-6 years depending on type
- **Practical impact**: Advisors who forward our emails or screenshot our templates for their records will need to archive those. Our emails should be professional enough to survive regulatory review.

### Recommended Archiving for Advisor Intelligence
- Archive all sent email campaigns (HTML + plain text)
- Log all send events with recipient lists
- Maintain unsubscribe request records indefinitely
- Keep records of all promotional offers and their terms
- Store email templates with version history
- Use Resend's built-in analytics and logs as primary record

# IMPORTANT: Technology Vendor vs. Investment Adviser Distinction

## What Advisor Intelligence IS
- A SaaS technology platform
- A provider of AI prompt templates
- A software tool for financial advisors
- A technology vendor subject to CAN-SPAM, FTC regulations, and general business laws

## What Advisor Intelligence IS NOT
- A registered investment adviser (RIA)
- A broker-dealer
- A financial planning firm
- A compliance consultancy

## How This Changes Compliance Requirements

| Requirement | Investment Advisers | Advisor Intelligence (Tech Vendor) |
|------------|--------------------|------------------------------------|
| SEC Marketing Rule | Fully applies | Does NOT apply directly |
| FINRA Rule 2210 | Applies to BD-affiliated | Does NOT apply directly |
| CAN-SPAM | Applies | Fully applies |
| FTC Act (Section 5) | Applies | Applies (no deceptive practices) |
| State consumer protection | Applies | Applies |
| Email archiving (SEC/FINRA) | Required (6+ years) | Not required, but recommended |
| Testimonial disclosures | Required by SEC rule | Not required, but best practice |
| Performance claims | Heavily regulated | Subject to FTC truthfulness standards |

## Bottom Line
Our emails are marketing software, not investment advice. Our primary compliance obligation is CAN-SPAM and FTC truthfulness standards. However, because our customers are regulated, we should maintain a higher standard than typical SaaS — our content should be "advisor-safe" and never create compliance problems for the advisors who use our product.
