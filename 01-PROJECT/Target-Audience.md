## Primary Users: Financial Advisors

### Firm Types
| Type | Description | AI Restrictions |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Independent RIAs** | Registered Investment Advisors, fiduciary standard, SEC/state regulated | Most flexible — no mandatory pre-approval for AI content |
| **Wirehouses** | Large firms (Merrill, Morgan Stanley, UBS, Wells) | Strictest — FINRA 2210 pre-approval required, conservative language only |
| **Broker-Dealers (IBDs)** | Independent broker-dealers (LPL, Raymond James, Cetera) | FINRA 2210 for retail comms, balanced language |
| **Hybrid RIA/BD** | Dual-registered firms | Apply MORE restrictive standard |
| **Solo Practitioners** | One-person advisory shops | Resource-constrained, most time savings |
| **Family Offices** | Multi-generational wealth management | Complex needs, high AUM |
| **Multi-Advisor Teams** | Ensemble practices | Need consistency across advisors |

### AUM Ranges Served
- Under $50M (emerging advisors)
- $50M–$250M (established practices)
- $250M–$1B (growth-stage firms)
- $1B+ (large firms and family offices)

### Pain Points (Why They Buy)
1. **Meeting prep** — Spending 1+ hour prepping for every hour of client meetings (no system generates meeting briefs)
2. **Post-meeting follow-ups** — 30-60 minutes per meeting to write follow-up email + CRM note (no system writes these)
3. **Email drafting** — Every client email takes 15-25 minutes from scratch (43% already use AI for this)
4. **Time on admin** — 22.1 hours/week (41.4%) on admin tasks, much of it writing
5. **Content creation** — LinkedIn, newsletters, blog posts — most advisors skip entirely
6. **Prospecting outreach** — Writing cold emails, COI letters, referral requests from scratch
7. **Client education** — Explaining complex concepts in accessible language (planning software generates jargon, not plain language)
8. **Compliance fears** — Worried about using AI wrong in a regulated industry

### What They Will NOT Use AI For (key insight from coaching sessions)
- Portfolio construction — they have Orion, Black Diamond, Envestnet
- Tax analysis — they have Holistiplan, FP Alpha
- Financial planning — they have eMoney, MoneyGuidePro, RightCapital
- Risk assessment — they have Riskalyze/Nitrogen
- Performance reporting — custodian systems handle this
- Compliance monitoring — compliance departments and RegEd

Templates for these functions **hurt credibility**. See [[Product-Strategy-Pivot]].

### What They Value
- **Trust** — Won't use tools that feel sketchy or unproven
- **Credibility** — Want to see finance-specific knowledge, not generic SaaS
- **ROI** — Need to see time savings in concrete terms (hours/week)
- **Ease of use** — Zero learning curve, copy-paste model
- **Compliance safety** — Regulatory references give confidence
- **Professional tone** — Output must sound like them, not a robot

### How They Find Tools
- Word of mouth (advisor communities, study groups)
- LinkedIn (primary social platform for advisors)
- Industry conferences (T3, Schwab IMPACT, Riskalyze)
- Custodian ecosystems (Schwab, Fidelity, Pershing)
- Advisor-focused media (Kitces, RIA Intel, InvestmentNews)

### Credentials They Hold
CFP®, CFA®, ChFC®, CIMA®, AIF®, CLU®, RICP®, WMCP®

### CRM/Tech They Use
- Redtail, Wealthbox, Salesforce Financial Services Cloud
- MoneyGuidePro, eMoney, RightCapital (planning software)
- Microsoft 365 / Copilot (primary AI tool for many firms)
- Riskalyze/Nitrogen, Holistiplan, Orion, Black Diamond
