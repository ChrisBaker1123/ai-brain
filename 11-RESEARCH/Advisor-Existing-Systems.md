# Advisor Existing Systems — What Software Advisors Already Have

> **Purpose**: Map every major advisory function to the existing system(s) that handle it. This tells us where AI adds value (gaps) vs. where it's redundant (covered). Any template we build that overlaps with these systems hurts our credibility.

---

## The Advisor Technology Stack

### Average Firm Stats (Kitces 2025)
- Average advisory firm uses **12 software applications** to serve **20 business functions**
- Firms spend **4-6% of annual revenue** on technology
- Technology satisfaction declined in **20 of 23 categories** (2025 Kitces Report)
- Integration quality is the **#1 driver** of technology satisfaction

## Function-by-Function System Map

### 1. Portfolio Construction & Management
**Status**: FULLY COVERED — regulated, audited, integrated

| System | Market Position | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| Orion Portfolio Solutions | Enterprise leader ($5.8T AUA) | AI assistant "Denali" launching |
| Black Diamond (SS&C) | Mid-large RIA standard | Comprehensive reporting + client portal |
| Envestnet | Largest platform ($6.3T AUA) | Research, portfolio construction, reporting |
| Tamarac (Envestnet) | Leading rebalancer | CRM + rebalancing + reporting |
| Riskalyze/Nitrogen | Risk-based portfolio construction | 30K advisors |

**AI adds value?** NO. These systems are regulated, audited, and custodian-integrated. Advisors cannot and will not delegate portfolio decisions to AI.

### 8. CRM (Client Relationship Management)
**Status**: COVERED for data storage, GAP for content creation

| System | Market Position | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| Redtail | Most popular among RIAs | Simple, affordable, API-friendly |
| Wealthbox | Growing challenger | Modern UI, workflow automation |
| Salesforce Financial Services Cloud | Enterprise/wirehouses | Most powerful, most complex |
| Junxure (AdvisorEngine) | Legacy, declining | Older platform |

**AI adds value?** YES — CRMs store data but don't **write** the meeting notes, don't **draft** the follow-up emails, don't **generate** the meeting prep briefs. **This is a major gap.**

---

### 9. Meeting Notes & Documentation
**Status**: PARTIALLY COVERED — new category, rapid growth

| System | Market Position | Pricing | Notes |
|--------|----------------|---------|-------|
| Jump | Category leader, 27K advisors | $75-175/mo | $80M Series B, records meetings |
| Zocks | #2, privacy-first | $130-150/mo | $45M Series B, no recording |
| Pulse360 | Boutique niche | $49-79/mo | Meeting lifecycle, batch processing |

**AI adds value?** YES — these tools cover recording and transcription, but many advisors refuse recording for compliance reasons. Even with recording tools, **meeting PREP** is uncovered. And CRM note **writing** from non-recorded meetings is a wide-open gap.

---

### 10. Client Communication & Email Drafting
**Status**: GAP — no dedicated system exists

No dedicated system helps advisors write client emails. CRMs store emails after they're sent. Email platforms (Outlook, Gmail) provide no content assistance. Marketing tools (Mailchimp, Constant Contact) handle bulk sends but not personalized client emails.

**AI adds value?** **ABSOLUTELY — this is our primary opportunity.** 43% of advisors already use AI for email drafting (Horsesmouth 2025). No existing system fills this gap.

---

### 11. Marketing & Content Creation
**Status**: GAP — no advisor-specific system exists

Some advisors use generic tools (Canva, Mailchimp, Hootsuite) but no advisor-specific content creation system exists. Most advisors either skip marketing entirely or outsource it.

**AI adds value?** **YES — second major opportunity.** LinkedIn content, newsletters, blog posts, seminar materials — no system creates advisor-appropriate content.

---

### 12. Prospecting & Business Development
**Status**: GAP — no dedicated system for outreach copy

CRMs track prospect pipeline. Some have email sequencing. But none help write the actual outreach messages, COI letters, or referral requests.

**AI adds value?** **YES — third major opportunity.**

### 13. Client Education Materials

Planning software generates reports full of financial jargon. No system takes complex financial concepts and translates them into client-friendly explanations.

**AI adds value?** **YES — fourth major opportunity.**

## Wirehouse vs. Independent RIA Technology

### Wirehouse Advisors (Morgan Stanley, Merrill Lynch, Wells Fargo, UBS)
Wirehouses provide **mandated platforms** for most functions:
- Portfolio management: Firm platform (not optional)
- Financial planning: Firm-provided (often eMoney or proprietary)
- CRM: Firm-mandated (often Salesforce)
- Compliance: Firm compliance department (strict pre-approval)
- Trading: Firm platform only
- Reporting: Firm system only

**Gap for wirehouse advisors**: Communication, meeting prep, prospecting copy. They have LESS flexibility to choose tools, so the gaps are even more acute. They can't easily add Jump or Zocks. But they CAN use copy-paste templates with Copilot (which their firm already pays for).

### Independent RIAs
Choose their own stack across 12+ categories. More flexibility, more integration headaches. Technology satisfaction is declining even as adoption increases.

**Gap for independent advisors**: Same communication gaps, plus practice management documentation. They have more freedom to choose tools but also more "gaps between tools."
