## Key Competitive Insights

### 1. The "Communication Gap" Is Wide Open

Every single competitor analyzed focuses on one of these workflows:
- **Document analysis**: Holistiplan, FP Alpha, VRGL (upload docs → get insights)
- **Meeting automation**: Jump, Zocks, Pulse360 (attend meetings → get notes/actions)
- **Financial planning**: RightCapital, eMoney, Conquest, Orion (input data → get plans)
- **Risk assessment**: Nitrogen/Riskalyze, Kwanti (assess risk → get proposals)
- **Custodial operations**: Altruist/Hazel (manage accounts → get AI assistance)

**None of them help advisors with content creation, client communication templates, prospecting copy, marketing materials, or educational content.** This is Advisor Intelligence's entire moat. Every competitor is a potential complement, not a replacement.

### 2. Pricing Tier Map — Advisor Intelligence Owns the Bottom

```
$50/mo:  ★ ADVISOR INTELLIGENCE ($49.99) ★ | Pulse360 ($49-79)
$100/mo: Nitrogen Riskalyze ($99) | RightCapital ($125-150)
$150/mo: Zocks ($130-150) | Jump ($75-175 avg $120)
$200/mo: Conquest ($200) | Kwanti ($195-575)
$300/mo: Orion Risk Intelligence ($275)
$400/mo: eMoney (~$375)
$500+/mo: VRGL (enterprise) | Orion bundles (custom)
Annual:  Holistiplan ($449-$30K/yr) | FP Alpha ($1,790-$1,995/yr)
```

At $49.99/mo, Advisor Intelligence is the cheapest dedicated AI tool for financial advisors. The only product at a similar price point (Pulse360 at $49/mo) is a meeting notes tool with a completely different value proposition.

### 4. The "Big Three" Planning Platforms Are Adding AI — But Slowly

eMoney (CoPlanner), RightCapital (Smart Import), and Conquest (SAM) are all adding AI features, but they're incremental additions to complex platforms. None are rethinking the advisor workflow from scratch. Advisor Intelligence's advantage: we're AI-native, not AI-bolted-on.

Most competitors rely on product-led growth and conference presence, not content marketing. Advisor Intelligence can win SEO with consistent, high-quality blog content about AI prompts for advisors — a keyword space that no competitor owns.

### 6. LinkedIn Presence Doesn't Correlate with Product Success

Nitrogen has 27K followers but declining growth. Jump has 251 followers but 27K users. LinkedIn follower count is vanity — product adoption is what matters. However, for Advisor Intelligence, LinkedIn is the primary discovery channel for financial advisors, so investing in LinkedIn content is strategic.
